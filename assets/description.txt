This haiku presents a river caught between movement and pause, where the "hurrying tumbling waves" suggest energy, urgency, and constant change. At the same time, the line "Nor stillness so cold" implies that the river is not frozen or lifeless, but alive and flowing. The poem captures a delicate balance in nature: neither complete motion nor absolute stillness, but a shifting in-between state. As a literary image, it can also suggest the emotions of uncertainty, transition, or being suspended between two conditions.
