This haiku captures a moment of youthful innocence and anticipation. The first line, 'He ran up to her,' suggests a sense of urgency or excitement, possibly indicating a meeting or reunion. The second line, 'The sun had fully risen,' sets the scene in the early morning, symbolizing new beginnings or enlightenment. The final line, 'In sight of the boy,' emphasizes the perspective of a young observer, perhaps highlighting the purity and simplicity of the moment. The haiku beautifully encapsulates a fleeting interaction under the fresh light of day, seen through the eyes of a child.
