This haiku captures a moment of uneasy self-awareness and social judgment. The opening word, "unfortunately," drops us straight into regret, as if the speaker is already bracing for blame. The second line—"looking very like a thief"—suggests that identity here is not about what someone has done, but how they appear to others; it hints at prejudice, profiling, and the fragile power of first impressions. The final line, "would you believe it," reads like a defensive aside to the reader, mixing disbelief with a wry, embarrassed humour. Altogether, the poem compresses a small personal crisis into three lines: the speaker’s sense of injustice, the absurdity of being misread, and the quiet sting of being judged by surfaces rather than truth.
