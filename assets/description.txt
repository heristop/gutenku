This haiku captures a moment of eager expectation and boundless hope. The phrase 'Here it comes again' suggests a recurring event or feeling, something familiar yet always exciting. 'As I verily believe' conveys a deep, almost spiritual conviction, emphasizing the speaker's unwavering faith in what is to come. The final line, 'It would soar cloud-high,' uses vivid imagery to illustrate the magnitude of this anticipated event or emotion, likening it to something that can rise to the heavens. The haiku beautifully encapsulates the essence of anticipation and the uplifting power of belief.
