This haiku captures a quiet coastal morning through delicate, sensory images. “The kiss of a wave” personifies the sea, suggesting a gentle, intimate touch rather than a crashing force—an atmosphere of tenderness and calm. The middle line, “though it was still so early,” places the scene at dawn, when the world feels hushed and half-awake, heightening the reader’s attentiveness to small movements and sounds. In the final line, “the pouched birds waddling,” the focus shifts from water to wildlife: the phrase evokes penguins or pelicans, their comic, grounded gait contrasting with the wave’s soft grace. That contrast—between the sea’s fluid motion and the birds’ awkward, endearing shuffle—creates a balanced portrait of morning life at the shore, where beauty includes both elegance and humble ordinariness.
