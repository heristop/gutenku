This haiku captures the essence of potential and the beauty that exists in both the grand and the minute aspects of life. The phrase 'Has yet to be made' suggests a sense of anticipation and creation, hinting at the endless possibilities that lie ahead. The 'beautiful things' and 'things great and small' encompass the vast spectrum of experiences and wonders that the world offers. This haiku invites readers to appreciate the present moment while acknowledging the untapped potential that surrounds us, encouraging a mindful awareness of both the seen and unseen beauty in life.
