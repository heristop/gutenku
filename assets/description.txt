This haiku reads like a quiet confession, spoken with careful restraint. The opening word, "particularly," signals a narrowing of focus: the speaker is not making a broad claim, but isolating a specific instance that still matters. In the second line, "As it had happened to me," the tone turns personal and reflective, suggesting experience as the basis for judgment rather than rumor or theory. The final line, "to give him his due," resolves into an ethical impulse—an insistence on fairness, even if it arrives belatedly. Overall, the poem captures a moment of moral clarity: the speaker recognizes that someone ("him") deserves acknowledgment, and the act of granting that recognition becomes the poem’s central gesture. Its power lies in understatement, letting a small phrase carry the weight of accountability and respect.
