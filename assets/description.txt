This haiku captures a moment of spiritual reflection and surrender. The speaker acknowledges a divine presence in which all things exist, suggesting a sense of omnipresence and omnipotence. The phrase 'that I might repose on Thee' indicates a desire for rest and trust in this higher power, implying a relinquishment of burdens and a search for peace. The concluding line, 'Thou hast done to-day,' suggests a recognition of the divine's active role in the events of the day, highlighting a sense of gratitude and acceptance. The haiku encapsulates a moment of serene faith and the comfort found in divine providence.
