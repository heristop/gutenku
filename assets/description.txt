This haiku creates a tense and unsettling image of helplessness and fear. The repeated line, "falling on the ground," frames the poem like a cycle or a loop, suggesting both physical collapse and emotional defeat. In the middle, "pursued by a pack of wolves" introduces danger, urgency, and instinctive survival. The wolves can be read literally as predators, or symbolically as threats, pressures, or fears that chase the speaker. The repetition at the end reinforces the sense that escape is impossible, making the poem feel trapped, frantic, and bleak. Its simplicity gives it power, leaving the reader with a stark impression of vulnerability and pursuit.
