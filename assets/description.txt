This haiku creates a light, dreamlike mood through the phrase "she walked on air," suggesting that Rowena feels joyful, excited, or almost unreal in her happiness. "Rowena was in the clouds" continues this sense of elevation, showing her thoughts lifted away from ordinary life. The final line, "the twins in advance," adds a surprising twist, hinting that the twins are ahead of time or perhaps arriving before expected, which gives the poem a slightly playful or mysterious ending. Overall, the haiku blends wonder, motion, and anticipation in a brief but vivid image.
