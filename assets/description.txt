This haiku reflects on the enduring and protective nature of the sun. The first line, 'To save our present,' suggests a desire to preserve the current state of the world or our lives. The second line, 'The second power above,' positions the sun as a mighty force, perhaps second only to a higher spiritual or cosmic power. Finally, 'The unwearied sun' emphasizes the sun's tireless and constant presence, symbolizing hope, continuity, and life. The haiku captures the sun's role as a steadfast guardian of our existence, highlighting its importance in sustaining life and maintaining balance in the natural world.
