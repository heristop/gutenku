This haiku captures a brief but powerful scene of surprise and awareness. The phrase "turned slowly around" creates a sense of suspense, as if something important is about to be revealed. "On seeing the young man" suggests a sudden shift in attention, while "Cromwell raised his head" gives the moment dignity and alertness, as though the figure named Cromwell responds with authority or curiosity. The poem is spare and direct, but it invites the reader to imagine the relationship between the two figures and the significance of the encounter. As in much haiku, the meaning lies not only in what is said, but in the silence and tension between the lines.
