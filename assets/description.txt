This haiku captures the essence of a profound connection between the human form and the celestial power of the sun. The phrase 'Wedged in one body' suggests a unity or coexistence of two forces within a single entity, possibly hinting at the human capacity to embody or channel greater powers. 'The second power above' refers to the sun, a symbol of energy, life, and constancy, which is often revered as a source of inspiration and strength. The 'unwearied sun' emphasizes its tireless nature, suggesting an eternal and unwavering presence. This haiku invites reflection on the relationship between humanity and the natural world, highlighting the potential for inner strength and resilience inspired by the sun's enduring energy.
