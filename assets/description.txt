This haiku sketches a small, eerie moment of impatience and dull routine. The opening—“Three of them bored him”—suggests a speaker trapped among others (perhaps companions, tasks, or repeated encounters) that feel numbing rather than engaging. The phrase “to be drugged with poppies” draws on the poppy’s literary associations with opium, sleep, and forgetfulness, implying that boredom acts like a narcotic: it sedates the mind, dulls perception, and makes time feel heavy. The final line—“When the half-hour struck”—personifies time with the language of a clock tower, as if the passing of thirty minutes lands with a cold, mechanical certainty. Together, the images create a tension between inner stupor and the external, relentless measurement of time: even when consciousness feels hazy, the clock still advances, marking a minor interval as though it were a significant event.
