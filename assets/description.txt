This haiku captures a moment of serene contemplation amidst the mundane pleasures of life. The opening line, 'Which to attend to,' suggests a moment of decision or reflection, prompting the reader to consider the choices that demand their attention. The second line, 'A beautifully calm race,' juxtaposes the idea of a race—often associated with haste and competition—with the tranquility of beauty, implying that even in a hurried life, one can find peace. Finally, 'Taking up biscuits' introduces a delightful, everyday activity that evokes warmth and comfort, inviting us to savor simple joys. Overall, the haiku encourages mindfulness and appreciation of the small, yet significant aspects of existence.
