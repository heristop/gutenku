This haiku captures anxiety as something both hidden and profound. The opening phrase, “as well as in low,” suggests a lowered state—emotionally, socially, or even physically—implying that distress can settle beneath the surface where it is less visible. The central question, “how deep is his anxiety,” turns inward and measures fear not by volume but by depth, emphasizing its weight and persistence. The final line, “The facility,” introduces an unsettling ambiguity: it may refer to an institution (a hospital, clinic, or care home) that contains and manages anxiety, or it may point to the mind’s own ‘facility’—its capacity to produce worry and self-surveillance. Together, the lines create a stark portrait of quiet dread: anxiety as a submerged force, intensified by containment and the cold, impersonal language of systems.
