This haiku captures a sudden, visceral awakening of power. “Deep in the muscle” suggests something primal and bodily—strength that exists beneath thought, stored in the body like instinct. The middle line, “summoned by his awful shout,” introduces a harsh external trigger: a cry that is frightening, commanding, or violent, capable of calling that buried force to the surface. The final image, “Tall and broad-shouldered,” completes the transformation by presenting a figure defined by physical dominance—either the person who has been roused into imposing stature or the threatening presence whose shout provokes the response. Overall, the poem compresses a moment of intimidation and instinctive readiness, where sound becomes the catalyst for embodied, almost animal strength.
