This haiku captures a moment of serene observation, where the speaker is engaged in watching something with great interest. The first line, 'Calmly looking on,' sets a tranquil tone, suggesting a peaceful and unhurried state of mind. The second line, 'With exceeding interest,' indicates that what is being observed holds significant fascination or curiosity for the observer. The final line, 'It may be worth while,' hints at the potential value or insight that might be gained from this moment of contemplation. Overall, the haiku reflects the beauty of mindful observation and the possibility of discovering something meaningful in the seemingly ordinary.
