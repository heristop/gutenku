This haiku explores the theme of tranquility and the profound peace that can be found in certainty. The opening line, 'There is to be had,' suggests an opportunity or a state of being that can be accessed, hinting at the idea that peace is attainable. The phrase 'In an assured kind of way' emphasizes a sense of confidence and stability, which enhances the notion that quietude is not merely the absence of noise, but a deliberate state of calm. Finally, 'Then a great quiet' encapsulates the essence of the haiku, presenting a serene conclusion that invites the reader to reflect on the value of inner stillness and the comforting embrace of silence. Overall, the haiku conveys a message of finding solace in certainty and the beauty of a quiet mind.
