This haiku captures the essence of beauty and acceptance. The first line, 'Fair to look upon,' sets the scene with an image of something visually pleasing, suggesting an immediate attraction or admiration. The second line, 'This mentally decided,' implies a conscious choice or judgment made by the observer, highlighting the power of perception and decision-making in determining what is considered beautiful. The final line, 'Where thou art welcome,' suggests a sense of belonging or acceptance, indicating that beauty, once recognized and appreciated, finds its place in the heart or mind of the observer. The haiku explores the interplay between perception, decision, and acceptance, inviting readers to reflect on how they perceive and welcome beauty in their own lives.
