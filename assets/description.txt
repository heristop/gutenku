This haiku sketches a quiet, accumulating despair. The opening line—“things are going badly”—drops us into crisis without explanation, creating a stark, conversational immediacy. The phrase “the old one still extant” suggests a lingering presence: an outdated habit, an unresolved grief, a former relationship, or even an older self that refuses to disappear. “Extant” is notably formal, giving the persistence of the past a cold, factual weight, as if the speaker is forced to acknowledge it like an unwelcome document still on file. The final line—“As the weeks went by”—stretches the moment into duration, implying that the trouble is not sudden but sustained, worsening through repetition and time. Together, the poem captures how unresolved remnants of the past can quietly sabotage the present, and how time, instead of healing, can sometimes simply lengthen the ache.
