This haiku captures a fleeting yet poignant moment of connection. The first line, 'He ran up to her,' sets the scene with a sense of urgency and anticipation. The second line, 'The sun had fully risen,' situates the event in the early morning, symbolizing new beginnings and clarity. The final line, 'In sight of the boy,' adds a layer of innocence and wonder, suggesting that this moment is being witnessed through the eyes of a child. The haiku beautifully encapsulates a brief, yet significant, interaction under the fresh light of a new day.
