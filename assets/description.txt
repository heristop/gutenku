This haiku paints a vivid image of a walrus in a moment of alertness and curiosity. The phrase 'His right arm thrown back' suggests a posture of readiness or perhaps a gesture of surprise. The walrus, a creature often associated with the cold, harsh environments of the Arctic, is depicted as actively surveying its surroundings. The line 'The walrus looks about him' emphasizes this sense of awareness and vigilance. Finally, 'The eyes are large' highlights the walrus's keen observation, suggesting a depth of perception and intelligence. This haiku captures a brief yet powerful moment in nature, inviting readers to appreciate the quiet intensity of the walrus's gaze.
