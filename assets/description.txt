This haiku captures a moment of profound realization and connection to the divine. The phrase 'This hath just its time' suggests a fleeting yet significant moment, emphasizing the transient nature of existence. The 'light-surrounded being' evokes imagery of enlightenment or purity, possibly representing the human spirit or a higher consciousness. The final line, 'Thou lofty heaven,' addresses the heavens or a divine presence, highlighting the relationship between the earthly and the celestial. Overall, the haiku reflects themes of temporality, transcendence, and the search for meaning beyond the physical realm.
