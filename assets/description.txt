This haiku captures the serene and rejuvenating essence of a morning by the sea. The first line, 'They praised the morning,' suggests a collective appreciation for the dawn, a time often associated with new beginnings and hope. The second line, 'The sea-air always does good,' emphasizes the invigorating and healing qualities of the ocean breeze, which is known to refresh both body and spirit. The final line, 'The astonishment,' encapsulates the awe and wonder that such a simple yet profound experience can evoke. The haiku beautifully conveys how nature's everyday miracles can leave us in a state of amazement and gratitude.
