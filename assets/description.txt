This haiku captures a tense inner struggle. The phrase "with a forced calmness" suggests someone trying to appear composed, but the calm is unnatural and difficult to maintain. "to all action or feeling" implies that both movement and emotion are being suppressed, as if the speaker is trying to hold everything inside. The final line, "Knocking still louder," creates a powerful contrast: even while silence is forced, the pressure of emotion or urgency grows stronger. The haiku conveys the feeling of restrained distress, where what is hidden becomes even more intense.
