This haiku presents a serene and inviting image that emphasizes both physical beauty and a sense of belonging. The first line, 'Fair to look upon,' suggests an appreciation for external appearances, hinting at aesthetic pleasure. The second line, 'This mentally decided,' introduces a reflective element, implying that beauty is not merely superficial but a conscious choice or perception. The final line, 'Where thou art welcome,' creates a sense of warmth and hospitality, reinforcing the idea that true beauty lies not only in appearance but also in the comfort and acceptance one feels in a particular place. Together, the lines evoke a harmonious blend of visual elegance and emotional resonance, inviting the reader to consider the deeper meanings of beauty and belonging.
