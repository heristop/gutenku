This haiku captures a profound sense of unity and spiritual elevation. The first line, 'That in Thee we live,' suggests a deep connection with a divine presence, emphasizing that our existence is intertwined with something greater. The second line, 'In the heights all Thy angels,' evokes imagery of celestial beings residing in elevated realms, symbolizing purity and enlightenment. The final line, 'Rejoiced in spirit,' conveys a sense of joy and spiritual fulfillment. Together, these lines create a vision of harmony between the divine, the celestial, and the human spirit, celebrating the interconnectedness of all existence.
