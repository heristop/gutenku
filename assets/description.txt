This haiku captures a moment of realization and connection with the world through sound. The phrase 'Just about this time' suggests a specific moment of inspiration or change, possibly reflecting the transition of seasons or a personal journey. The line 'It was heard in every sound' conveys the idea that at this moment, the speaker becomes acutely aware of the beauty and significance of their surroundings, suggesting a deepening appreciation for life's nuances. Finally, 'And learned how to write' implies that this heightened awareness has inspired the speaker to express themselves creatively, indicating that the act of listening and observing has led to the ability to articulate thoughts and emotions through writing. Overall, the haiku encapsulates the transformative power of perception and the relationship between nature, sound, and creativity.
