This haiku captures a tense, private confrontation in three swift brushstrokes. The first line, "as he looked at me," establishes a direct gaze that feels accusatory, placing the speaker under scrutiny. The second line intensifies the moment: "still in a towering rage" personifies anger as something physically immense, suggesting it dominates the space and threatens to overwhelm both people. The final line, "taking up my hat," is a small, domestic action that carries symbolic weight—an understated gesture of withdrawal, self-protection, or departure. The contrast between the ordinary hat and the extraordinary rage highlights how conflict can erupt into everyday life, and how the speaker’s quiet movement becomes a way of reclaiming control amid intimidation.
