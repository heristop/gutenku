This haiku captures a moment of urgency and transition, set against the backdrop of late October. The phrase 'Make haste and come in' suggests a call to action, perhaps urging someone to seek shelter or join in a gathering before it's too late. The mention of October evokes the end of autumn, a time when days grow shorter and colder, hinting at the impending arrival of winter. 'During all that time' reflects on the passage of time, suggesting a period of waiting or anticipation. The haiku encapsulates a sense of urgency, change, and reflection, characteristic of the seasonal shift from autumn to winter.
