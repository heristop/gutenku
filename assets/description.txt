This haiku captures a moment of profound tranquility and inner peace, likening it to the calm demeanor of a victorious leader. The phrase 'Calm with gentle soul' suggests a deep, serene presence, while 'Of victor chiefs preëminent' evokes the image of a leader who has achieved greatness and now rests in quiet confidence. The mention of 'Kausalyá Calmed' could be a reference to a figure from ancient texts, symbolizing wisdom and composure. Overall, the haiku reflects the idea that true victory is not just in conquest but in the peace that follows.
