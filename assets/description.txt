This haiku evokes a sense of value, repetition, and the quiet pressure of time. The image of a "gold coin" suggests wealth, worth, or something precious, while "the annual coinage" implies a recurring process of making or measuring value each year. The phrase "must / at an average" feels deliberately clipped and unfinished, creating a formal, almost mechanical tone. As a whole, the poem can be read as reflecting on how value is standardized, counted, and controlled by yearly cycles, rather than existing as something purely natural or personal. Its sparse language leaves room for ambiguity, inviting the reader to think about economics, time, and the uneasy relationship between individuality and average measure.
