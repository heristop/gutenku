This haiku captures a quiet but tense moment in nature. The phrase "set by a current" suggests something being carried or positioned by the flowing water, while "fast sleeping on the water" creates a calm, almost dreamlike image of stillness. However, this peace is disrupted by "a rising gale," which hints that a storm is approaching. The poem contrasts calm and movement, sleep and danger, creating a sense of suspense. Like many haiku, it uses simple language to evoke a vivid natural scene and an emotional atmosphere.
