This haiku presents a scene of peaceful contemplation, where the speaker is faced with a choice between two serene activities. The first line, 'Which to attend to,' suggests a moment of decision, highlighting the abundance of tranquility in the speaker's surroundings. The second line, 'A beautifully calm race,' juxtaposes the concept of a race, typically associated with speed and competition, with calmness, suggesting a leisurely, perhaps metaphorical, journey or activity. The final line, 'Taking up biscuits,' adds a touch of whimsy and domesticity, grounding the scene in a simple pleasure. This haiku captures the essence of finding beauty and peace in everyday choices, emphasizing the importance of savoring life's small, serene moments.
