This haiku captures a moment of serene observation, where the speaker reflects on the world's inherent beauty and the joy it brings. The phrase 'I think they like it' suggests a shared appreciation, possibly hinting at a connection between the speaker and others who find delight in the world's offerings. The use of 'very pleasant' emphasizes the simplicity and joy found in everyday experiences, while 'the whole big dear world' evokes a sense of tenderness and affection towards life and nature. Overall, the haiku conveys a sense of harmony and contentment with the world around us, inviting readers to pause and appreciate the beauty in their surroundings.
