This haiku captures a moment of earnest supplication to higher powers, emphasizing the centrality of love as the driving force behind the plea. The first line, 'Thy suppliant hear,' suggests a humble request for attention from a divine entity. The second line, 'Of all the powers above,' broadens the scope to include the celestial or spiritual realm, indicating that the speaker seeks favor or intervention from a higher authority. The final line, 'Thy love the motive,' reveals that love is the underlying reason for this appeal, suggesting that the speaker's intentions are pure and heartfelt. This haiku beautifully encapsulates the theme of love as a universal motivator in spiritual and personal supplications.
