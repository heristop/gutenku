This haiku captures the profound sense of abandonment and isolation experienced by the speaker. The imagery of a 'last friend' departing suggests a final severance of companionship, leaving the speaker in a state of desolation. The phrase 'to die alone' underscores the depth of their loneliness, evoking a sense of existential despair. The comparison to a 'weary child' highlights vulnerability and exhaustion, emphasizing the emotional toll of being left behind. This haiku poignantly conveys the universal fear of solitude and the longing for connection.
