This haiku captures a quiet return—both physical and emotional—in the simple phrase “making my way back.” The speaker’s movement suggests retracing steps, coming home, or revisiting a past self. In the middle line, “hiding it with the flowers,” nature becomes a veil: flowers symbolize beauty, gentleness, and seasonal change, but they also act as camouflage, softening or concealing whatever the speaker carries—perhaps a feeling, a mistake, or a private truth. The final line, “luckily for me,” introduces a subtle, relieved tone, implying the concealment has worked and the speaker has avoided exposure or judgment. Overall, the poem balances delicacy and secrecy, using the imagery of flowers to suggest that what is most personal can be tucked away in plain sight.
