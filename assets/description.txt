This haiku creates a hushed, mysterious city scene. The phrase "swung up on the steps" suggests a sudden or graceful movement, as if someone or something has risen into view. "Under the sleepy street-lamp" gives the setting a dim, late-night atmosphere, making the moment feel still and slightly dreamlike. The final line, "beside the left ear," is especially intriguing: it focuses on a small, intimate detail, which may imply closeness, whispering, or a subtle sensation rather than a full clear image. Overall, the poem blends movement, silence, and ambiguity, inviting the reader to imagine a fleeting urban moment filled with quiet tension and delicacy.
