This haiku opens with a quiet shift in atmosphere: “The fog was rising” suggests morning’s veil lifting, a gentle unveiling of the world. The second line pivots from sky to water, where movement becomes the focus—“so agile in the water” captures quick, effortless motion, as if the scene has suddenly come alive. The final line, “a kind of small duck,” grounds the poem in humble specificity: not a grand creature, but an ordinary, easily overlooked bird. That plain phrasing feels intentionally observational, like a naturalist’s note, and it heightens the haiku’s central contrast—soft, drifting fog above and brisk, purposeful life below. Overall, the poem offers a moment of calm attentiveness, where a subtle change in weather frames a small, vivid instance of vitality.
