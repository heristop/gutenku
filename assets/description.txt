This haiku reflects a spiritual meditation on the omnipresence and exalted nature of the divine, specifically referencing the Christian figure of the 'Son of man.' The opening line, 'Wherever they be,' suggests a universal reach, indicating that no matter where people are, they are under the watchful presence of the divine. The second line, 'In my Lord the Son of man,' directly identifies this divine presence with Jesus Christ, emphasizing a personal relationship and reverence. The final line, 'Oh how high art Thou,' expresses awe and admiration for the elevated status of the divine, suggesting both physical and spiritual height. The haiku captures a sense of wonder and reverence, highlighting the transcendence and omnipresence of the divine in the believer's life.
