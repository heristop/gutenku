This haiku captures a sudden, intimate moment of revelation. The opening image—“with an absent look”—suggests someone physically present yet mentally elsewhere, as if their attention is fixed on something beyond the immediate world. The second line shifts into a heightened, almost archaic address (“hast thou”), implying awe or gratitude: the person’s distant gaze has somehow “made” new stars visible, not literally, but by changing how the speaker sees—awakening wonder, hope, or a fresh perspective. The final line—“grasped him by the hands”—grounds the cosmic vision in human touch. It reads like an urgent attempt to anchor someone drifting away (into thought, grief, or destiny), or to share the revelation through connection. The tension between distance (absent look), transcendence (new stars), and closeness (hands) gives the poem its emotional charge: the universe opens, and the speaker reaches out to keep the moment—and the person—near.
