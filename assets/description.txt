This haiku moves from the quiet certainty of sunrise into a burst of communal celebration, then ends on a reflective, slightly enigmatic note. The opening image—“the sun from rising”—sets a scene of renewal and beginning, a classic haiku gesture toward nature’s cyclical rhythms. The middle line—“flower we sang splendidly”—personifies the moment: the speaker and others seem to sing with (or as) the flower, suggesting joy, growth, and a shared, almost ceremonial praise of life. The final line—“Rock mountain they think”—shifts the mood from lyrical immediacy to contemplation. The hard, enduring “rock mountain” contrasts with the delicate “flower,” and “they think” introduces distance: perhaps others interpret the scene differently, or perhaps the mountain stands as a symbol of permanence against fleeting beauty. Overall, the poem balances softness and solidity, song and silence, inviting us to consider how nature can be both experienced emotionally and judged intellectually.
