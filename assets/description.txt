This haiku paints a serene maritime portrait through vivid, contrasting imagery. “white as ivory” suggests a ship’s hull (or sail) rendered pure, polished, and almost statuesque—an emblem of elegance and stillness. The next line, “Her shining copper sheathing,” introduces warmth and craft: copper evokes the protective plating of a vessel, gleaming with care and history, and the pronoun “Her” gently personifies the ship as a dignified presence rather than a mere object. The final line, “and the sea was calm,” settles the scene into quiet equilibrium. The calm water becomes both setting and mood, allowing the bright whites and metallic shine to stand out, while also implying safety, contemplation, and a moment of poised tranquillity before any potential voyage or change.
