This haiku captures a sudden, tender moment of human vulnerability. The first line—“even made him cry”—drops us into emotion without context, suggesting that whatever has happened is powerful enough to break through pride or composure. In the second line, “Then he struggled to get up,” the poem shifts from inner feeling to physical difficulty, implying injury, exhaustion, age, or shock; the word “struggled” slows the rhythm and makes the scene feel heavy and real. The final line—“a complete stranger”—reframes everything: the speaker’s attention and empathy are directed toward someone unknown, turning the episode into a meditation on compassion and shared fragility. The haiku’s spare language leaves space for the reader to imagine the cause, while its emotional arc moves from tears, to effort, to recognition that suffering does not require familiarity to matter.
