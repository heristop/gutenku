This haiku encapsulates the harmonious relationship between man and nature, as suggested by its vivid imagery. The phrase 'The Greek obeys him' may refer to a figure representing nature or a divine entity, hinting at the reverence the Greeks had for their gods and the natural world. The 'snowy flowerets fair' evokes a sense of delicate beauty, possibly symbolizing purity and fragility. The final line, 'Proud of his deed,' suggests a sense of accomplishment and pride in the act of nurturing or appreciating this beauty. Overall, the haiku reflects themes of admiration, obedience to nature, and the pride that comes with recognizing one's place within it.
