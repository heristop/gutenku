This haiku suggests a quiet moment of discovery after absence and exhaustion. The opening line, "very out of breath," creates a sense of urgency and physical strain, as though the speaker has been searching or rushing. The next line, "The other man left last night," introduces a feeling of loss, separation, or mystery, implying that someone important has gone away. The final line, "finds the manuscript," brings a surprising turn: something hidden or meaningful is recovered. As a whole, the poem feels sparse but emotionally rich, hinting at departure, search, and the fragile recovery of words, memory, or truth.
