This haiku captures the essence of enduring relationships and the deep affection that binds individuals together. The first line, 'We were together,' sets the scene of unity and companionship. The second line, 'With great affection of love,' emphasizes the profound emotional connection shared among the individuals. The final line, 'Thy sons my masters,' suggests a sense of reverence and admiration, possibly indicating a relationship where the speaker learns from or is guided by the younger generation. This haiku beautifully encapsulates the timeless nature of love and respect across generations.
