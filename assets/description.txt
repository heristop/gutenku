This haiku explores the delicate balance between personal existence and the act of prayer for others. The first line, 'How to live my life,' suggests a reflective inquiry into one’s own existence and the choices made therein. The second line, 'In order to pray for them,' introduces a sense of purpose, indicating that the speaker’s actions are not solely for their own benefit but are directed towards the well-being of others. The final line, 'Peace and happiness,' encapsulates the desired outcomes of such prayers, highlighting themes of compassion and altruism. Overall, the haiku invites readers to consider how their lives can be a vessel for hope and positivity for those around them.
