This haiku presents a vivid scene where a Greek figure commands attention and respect, symbolized by the 'snowy flowerets fair.' The imagery of 'snowy flowerets' suggests purity and beauty, perhaps indicating that the Greek's influence is both gentle and profound. The phrase 'And thou be witness' invites the reader to observe this interaction, emphasizing the importance of witnessing acts of obedience and beauty. The haiku captures a moment of harmony between authority and nature, inviting reflection on the power of influence and the role of the observer.
