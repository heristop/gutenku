This haiku captures a moment of transition and opportunity. The first line, 'The tide had just turned,' suggests a change in circumstances, perhaps symbolizing a new beginning or a shift in perspective. The second line, 'The wood was pretty open,' evokes a sense of clarity and possibility, as if the path ahead is clear and inviting. The final line, 'With this to help me,' implies that the speaker feels supported or encouraged by these changes. Overall, the haiku reflects a harmonious relationship between nature and personal growth, where external changes inspire internal readiness.
