This haiku sets a stark scene of sound and menace. The opening image, “whistling in the wind,” suggests something thin, restless, and elusive—an atmosphere where nature itself seems to speak. The simile that follows, “Like that rapacious bird,” sharpens the tone: the wind’s whistle becomes predatory, hinting at hunger, threat, or opportunism circling overhead. The final phrase, “with a blazing brand,” introduces a sudden flare of heat and violence—“brand” evokes both a burning stick and the act of marking or claiming. Together, the images imply a force that not only hunts but also scorches and possesses, turning the natural wind into an emblem of danger, pursuit, and irreversible consequence.
