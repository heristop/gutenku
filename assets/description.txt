This haiku captures the essence of beauty and acceptance. The first line, 'Fair to look upon,' sets the scene with an image of something or someone visually appealing. The second line, 'This mentally decided,' suggests a conscious recognition or judgment of this beauty, highlighting the subjective nature of perception. The final line, 'Where thou art welcome,' implies that this beauty, once acknowledged, finds a place of acceptance and belonging. The haiku explores the interplay between perception and acceptance, suggesting that beauty, once recognized, naturally finds its place in the world.
