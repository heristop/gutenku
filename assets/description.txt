This haiku captures the quiet endurance of a person facing a major crisis. The phrase "through a great crisis" suggests a difficult, overwhelming period, while "waiting and growing stronger" reflects patience, resilience, and inner development during hardship. The final line, "so I built a fire," works as a powerful symbol: the fire can represent hope, warmth, survival, and self-made strength. As a whole, the poem suggests that even in uncertainty, one can endure, prepare, and create something sustaining from struggle.
