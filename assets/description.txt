This haiku captures a quiet, reflective moment charged with tension and ambiguity. The image of someone lighting a cigarette suggests pause, contemplation, or nervousness, while rising from the table implies a decision or departure. The final line, “If the thing was true,” introduces doubt and suspense, leaving the reader with a sense that something important has been heard, suspected, or revealed, but not yet confirmed. The poem’s power lies in its restraint: ordinary actions become meaningful because they are framed by uncertainty and emotional weight.
