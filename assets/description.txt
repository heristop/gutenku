This haiku captures a moment of serene beauty under the moonlight. The speaker seems to be addressing someone, perhaps inviting them to appreciate the scene together. The phrase 'If it will please you' suggests a gentle invitation or a desire to share this moment of tranquility. The 'brilliant moonlight' paints a vivid picture of a night illuminated by the moon's glow, creating an atmosphere of calm and wonder. The final line, 'As fair as can be,' emphasizes the purity and perfection of the scene, suggesting that the moonlit night is as beautiful as it can possibly be. This haiku encapsulates the simplicity and elegance of nature, inviting the reader to pause and appreciate the quiet beauty of the world around them.
