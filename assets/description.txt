This haiku captures the fleeting nature of beauty and moments in time. The phrase 'This hath just its time' suggests the transient quality of an experience or an emotion, emphasizing that everything has its moment before it passes. 'As with a double stillness' evokes a sense of profound calmness and reflection, perhaps indicating a pause to appreciate the beauty that is present. The final line, 'Veiled in thy beauty,' suggests that this beauty is subtle and perhaps hidden, requiring a deeper appreciation to fully understand its essence. The haiku invites readers to savor the quiet, transient moments of beauty that life offers, encouraging mindfulness and presence.
