This haiku reads like the opening of a scene: a speaker pauses—“Where shall we begin”—as if gathering an audience and preparing a shared moment. The second line, with its slightly archaic phrasing (“keep ope”), suggests eyes held open against drowsiness, evoking the struggle between sleep and attention. By ending with “let them come hither,” the poem becomes an invitation: draw closer, wake up, and take part. Overall, the haiku captures a gentle summoning from lethargy into presence—an awakening not only of the body, but of the mind, as though the act of beginning itself is what dispels sleep.
