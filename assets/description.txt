This haiku captures a moment of unexpected fortune and surprise in nature. The first line, 'Luckily for me,' sets a tone of gratitude or relief, suggesting that the speaker has narrowly avoided an unpleasant experience. The second line, 'It first buzzed over my head,' introduces an element of nature, likely an insect or bird, that has come close to the speaker, creating a sense of immediacy and tension. The final line, 'All at once she flushed,' suggests a sudden movement or change, possibly referring to a bird taking flight or a person blushing. This line adds a layer of surprise and beauty to the scene, transforming a potentially unsettling moment into one of wonder and appreciation. The haiku encapsulates the unpredictability and beauty of nature, highlighting how moments of discomfort can lead to unexpected joy.
