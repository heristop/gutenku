This haiku creates a quiet, sorrowful scene in which the horses’ restless pawing suggests unease or grief. Their gaze toward the moon gives the image a lonely, reflective mood, as if they are searching for comfort in the night sky. The final line, “picked a flower,” adds a gentle, surprising softness that can symbolize a brief act of tenderness, memory, or fragile beauty amid sadness. Together, the images blend nature, emotion, and stillness to evoke a deeply melancholic atmosphere.
