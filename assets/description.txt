This haiku captures the universal longing for rest and peace, personifying sleep as a cherished entity that all things desire. The first line, 'Especially those,' suggests a particular group or perhaps all beings who are in need of rest. The second line, 'That sleep may come unto him,' implies a wish or hope that sleep will find its way to the weary. The final line, 'All things long for thee,' broadens the scope, indicating that this yearning for rest is not limited to humans but extends to all of creation. The haiku beautifully encapsulates the idea that rest is a fundamental need shared by all living things, highlighting the gentle and nurturing nature of sleep.
