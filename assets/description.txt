This haiku captures a brief, intimate moment in which music becomes a form of quiet confession. “while the song lasted” frames the scene as fleeting, suggesting that whatever is revealed exists only within the song’s duration. The speaker then narrows our focus to “the one she most often played,” implying habit, comfort, and perhaps a repeated return to the same emotional landscape—she chooses this piece again and again because it suits what she cannot easily say. The final line, “for her music told,” completes the thought with gentle restraint: her feelings are communicated indirectly, through sound rather than speech. The haiku therefore explores how art can function as a private language, and how repetition can signal a persistent longing or memory. Even with its pared-down phrasing, it suggests a whole story of emotion expressed in performance rather than explanation.
