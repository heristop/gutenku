This haiku captures a moment of serene beauty under the moonlight. The speaker seems to be addressing someone, perhaps inviting them to appreciate the scene together. The phrase 'If it will please you' suggests a gentle invitation or a desire to share this moment of tranquility. The 'brilliant moonlight' sets the scene, casting a luminous glow that enhances the beauty of the night. The final line, 'As fair as can be,' emphasizes the perfection and grace of the moment, suggesting that the moonlit scene is as beautiful as it can possibly be. This haiku evokes a sense of peace and appreciation for the simple, yet profound beauty found in nature.
