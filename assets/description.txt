This haiku captures the essence of transformation and the inevitability of change. The opening line, 'There is to be had,' suggests the idea of potential or opportunity that exists within our experiences. The phrase 'Made over so many times' implies a process of renewal, where thoughts, emotions, or situations are revisited and reshaped through reflection and time. Finally, the line 'Then a great quiet' evokes a sense of peace or resolution that follows this continuous cycle of alteration, indicating that after the turmoil of change, one can find tranquility. Overall, this haiku invites readers to contemplate the nature of existence and the calm that can emerge from the chaos of life.
