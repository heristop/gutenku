This haiku captures a moment of introspection and reverence as the speaker prepares to leave a place of significance. The 'unconquered sea' symbolizes the vast, untamed forces of nature that remain beyond human control. By stating 'He comprehends all,' the poem suggests that the sea possesses an ancient wisdom and understanding that transcends human knowledge. The haiku evokes a sense of humility and awe, as the speaker acknowledges the enduring power and mystery of the natural world before departing.
