This haiku suggests a sudden, sharp moment of betrayal or suspicion. The phrase "darting forth a prong" creates an image of something quick, pointed, and possibly dangerous, as if an attack or accusation has emerged. "Turning to my guide his face" introduces a person who should be trusted, a guide, but the shift in attention makes the moment feel uneasy, as though the speaker is searching for reassurance. The final line, "vessel of all guile," reveals the true emotional center of the poem: the guide’s face becomes a container of deceit, suggesting that the person who seemed trustworthy is actually full of cunning and trickery. The haiku is brief but powerful, using vivid imagery and a dramatic turn to explore the theme of hidden dishonesty.
