This haiku presents a juxtaposition of serenity and choice. The first line, 'Which to attend to,' introduces a moment of decision, suggesting a scenario where multiple options vie for attention. The second line, 'A beautifully calm race,' evokes an image of a peaceful yet dynamic scene, perhaps a metaphorical race where the participants move with grace and tranquility. This line captures the essence of finding beauty in motion and calmness in competition. The final line, 'Taking up biscuits,' adds a whimsical touch, grounding the abstract with a simple, everyday action. It suggests a pause, a moment of indulgence or comfort amidst the choices and the calm race. Overall, the haiku reflects on the balance between decision-making, appreciating serene beauty, and finding joy in small pleasures.
