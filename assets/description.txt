This haiku conveys a tense, urgent mood. The speaker warns that something must happen quickly because there is fear of being stopped or prevented if the plan becomes known. The phrase "Caska be sodaine" suggests haste and immediacy, while "for we feare preuention" reveals anxiety about interference. The final line, "If this be knowne," intensifies the secrecy and danger, implying that knowledge itself could ruin the moment. Overall, the poem feels dramatic, suspenseful, and full of hidden intent.
