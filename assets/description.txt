This haiku captures a moment of emotional resilience and hope amidst adversity. The first line, 'Mate cheerful again,' suggests a return to a positive state of mind, possibly after a period of distress. The second line, 'The sea will save me from Him,' introduces the sea as a metaphorical refuge or savior from an oppressive force, personified as 'Him.' This could imply a struggle against a domineering presence or situation. The final line, 'Come good wind or foul,' reflects a determination to persevere regardless of circumstances, embracing both favorable and challenging conditions. The haiku, with its vivid imagery and emotional depth, conveys a sense of liberation and steadfastness in the face of life's unpredictable nature.
