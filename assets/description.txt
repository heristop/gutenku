This haiku captures a quiet act of looking beyond what is immediately visible. The opening phrase, “and looked in unseen,” suggests a deliberate search into hidden spaces—memory, shadow, or meanings that daylight conceals. “An hour or two after dark” places the speaker in a liminal time when the world is muted and perception becomes uncertain, inviting introspection as much as observation. The closing line, “though a little grey,” introduces ambiguity: the darkness is not absolute, and the grey hints at dim light, fading certainty, or a mood of gentle melancholy. Overall, the poem lingers on the tension between wanting clarity and accepting partial sight, using the night’s softened palette to evoke reflection and quiet longing.
