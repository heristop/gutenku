This haiku captures the essence of time's relentless progression. The opening line, 'When we speak of it,' suggests a reflective conversation about time itself, hinting at our human tendency to ponder its nature. The second line, 'We measure times as they pass,' emphasizes our innate desire to quantify and understand time, marking its passage through various means, whether through clocks, calendars, or personal milestones. The final line, 'To complete one day,' brings the focus to the cyclical nature of time, highlighting the completion of a single day as a microcosm of life's larger journey. The haiku invites readers to contemplate the fleeting yet significant moments that comprise our daily existence.
