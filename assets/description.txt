This haiku reflects a sense of contentment and appreciation for the beauty of existence. The speaker suggests that there is a universal enjoyment found in the world around us, hinting at a shared joy among all living beings. The phrase 'I think they like it' introduces a contemplative voice, pondering the feelings of others and connecting them to the broader human experience. The repetition of pleasantness in 'something very pleasant' reinforces the idea that life, with its myriad of experiences, is fundamentally enjoyable. The concluding line, 'The whole big dear world,' encapsulates a sense of warmth and affection towards the world, elevating it to 'dear' status. This haiku invites readers to pause and appreciate the simple yet profound beauty of life and connection with others.
