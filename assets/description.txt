This haiku creates a vivid, dreamlike winter scene where nature and human life seem to merge. The phrase "they froze into ice" suggests a sudden, complete transformation, as if everything has been overtaken by deep cold. "The building was far advanced" gives the impression of something old, weathered, or perhaps buried in time, while "of his hair the trees" is strikingly unusual and poetic, making the trees seem like an extension of a person’s body. This blending of images gives the haiku a surreal, contemplative mood, where the boundary between the man-made, the natural, and the human becomes blurred. Overall, the poem feels cold, still, and mysterious, like a frozen landscape suspended in silence.
