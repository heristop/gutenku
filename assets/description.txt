This haiku presents a speaker who has hidden their hardships—“concealed my struggles”—suggesting a practiced silence and an inward burden kept from view. Against that secrecy, “unalloyed inclination” introduces a striking contrast: a desire or impulse that is pure, unmixed, and sincere, as if the speaker’s core longing remains uncontaminated by pain. The final line, “by everything,” shifts the focus outward and widens the pressure: it implies that the speaker’s pure inclination is not merely challenged by one obstacle, but shaped, surrounded, or even overwhelmed by the totality of life—people, expectations, circumstances. The poem’s power lies in its tension between inner truth and external force: private suffering is hidden, authentic desire persists, yet the world in its fullness presses in, complicating what the heart wants.
