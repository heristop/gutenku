This haiku captures the essence of a memory or experience that returns to the mind with vivid clarity. The phrase 'It comes back to one' suggests a personal reflection or reminiscence that resurfaces. 'With undiminished clearness' implies that the memory retains its original vividness and impact, undeterred by the passage of time. The final line, 'Which is still alive,' indicates that this memory or experience continues to hold significance and vitality in the present moment. The haiku beautifully encapsulates the enduring power of memories and their ability to remain vibrant and influential in our lives.
