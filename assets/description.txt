This haiku creates a haunting, mysterious mood. The phrase "death-like silence" suggests an eerie stillness, as if nature itself has paused in reverence or fear. The image of a "variable star" introduces a celestial presence that is unstable or changing, which gives the poem a sense of uncertainty and quiet tension. By placing this star "loomed above the sea," the poet connects the vast sky and ocean, making the scene feel immense, lonely, and slightly ominous. Overall, the haiku uses stark imagery and silence to evoke awe, mystery, and a subtle sense of foreboding.
