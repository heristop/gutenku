This haiku reflects on the profound relationship between the speaker and the divine, specifically referencing 'my Lord the Son of man,' likely alluding to Jesus Christ. The first line, 'That I measure time,' suggests a personal metric of time influenced by faith and spirituality rather than conventional clocks. The second line deepens this sentiment by acknowledging the divine presence of the Son of man as a pivotal figure in the speaker's life. The closing line, 'Oh how high art Thou,' conveys reverence and awe, emphasizing the exalted nature of the divine. This haiku encapsulates a moment of introspection where time, faith, and divinity intertwine, prompting readers to consider their own measures of significance in life.
