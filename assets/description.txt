This haiku suggests a quiet passage between moments, as if something important happens just beyond the open door. The phrase "through the open door" creates a sense of invitation, movement, and transition, while "When the appointed night came" adds a feeling of fate or inevitability, as though the event was destined to arrive. The final line, "on the next morning," shifts the scene into daylight, creating a subtle contrast between night and morning, expectation and realization. Together, the lines evoke mystery, time passing, and the delicate boundary between what is awaited and what is revealed.
