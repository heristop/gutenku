This haiku captures the essence of duality and power within a single entity. The phrase 'Wedged in one body' suggests a unity or convergence of forces, possibly hinting at the human experience or a natural phenomenon. 'The second power above' introduces a hierarchy, implying that there is a greater force at play, perhaps the divine or cosmic order. Finally, 'The unwearied sun' symbolizes an eternal, unwavering source of energy and life. The sun, often seen as a symbol of constancy and vitality, reinforces the theme of enduring power and presence. This haiku invites readers to reflect on the interconnectedness of human existence and the greater forces that govern the universe.
