This haiku captures the essence of a sacred space where all creatures coexist in harmony. The phrase 'They live a real life' suggests authenticity and purity in the way these beings live. The second line, 'Of all creatures in the world,' emphasizes the inclusivity and diversity of life forms present in this place. Finally, 'The place is holy' elevates the setting to a spiritual level, suggesting that the natural world itself is a sanctuary deserving of reverence and respect.
