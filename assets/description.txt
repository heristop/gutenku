This haiku captures a moment of introspection and connection with the world. The speaker reflects on the act of naming, suggesting that they have found a fitting name for the vast and cherished world around them. The phrase 'the whole big dear world' conveys a sense of affection and wonder, emphasizing the speaker's appreciation and reverence for the world in its entirety. The haiku invites readers to consider the power of names and the personal significance they can hold, as well as the beauty and vastness of the world that surrounds us.
