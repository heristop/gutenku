This haiku captures a sense of joy and appreciation for the beauty of the world. The opening line, 'I think they like it,' suggests a contemplative voice reflecting on the perceptions of others, hinting at a shared experience of happiness. The phrase 'As something very pleasant' reinforces this idea, evoking a sense of contentment that can be found in nature and life itself. The concluding line, 'The whole big dear world,' encapsulates the vastness and warmth of the world, inviting readers to consider the collective joy of existence. Overall, the haiku invites a moment of reflection on how we perceive and interact with the world around us, emphasizing a universal appreciation for life's simple pleasures.
