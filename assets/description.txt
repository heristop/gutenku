This haiku captures a moment of collective appreciation and joy. The speaker observes a universal sense of enjoyment, suggesting that 'they'—perhaps people, animals, or even nature itself—find something delightful. The phrase 'the whole big dear world' evokes a sense of unity and affection, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all living things in experiencing this pleasantness. The haiku invites readers to reflect on the simple, shared pleasures that bind us together, transcending individual experiences to touch the broader tapestry of life.
