This haiku encapsulates a moment of introspection and enlightenment experienced by the speaker while bathing in a river. The imagery of bathing suggests a cleansing or renewal, while the river symbolizes the flow of thoughts and emotions. The phrase 'as it seemed to me' indicates a personal perspective, inviting readers to reflect on their own experiences of clarity and revelation. The connection between the act of bathing and 'intelligence' suggests that moments of stillness and connection with nature can lead to profound insights. Overall, this haiku invites contemplation on the relationship between nature, self-awareness, and understanding.
