This haiku poignantly addresses themes of empathy, racial identity, and inner strength. The first line, 'Do not weep for me,' suggests a plea for understanding rather than pity. The second line, 'Your skin is darker than mine,' acknowledges racial differences, highlighting the unique experiences and struggles that come with them. The final line, 'There is might in each,' unites the message by emphasizing that regardless of skin color, there is inherent strength and resilience in every individual. The haiku encourages mutual respect and recognition of each other's inner power.
