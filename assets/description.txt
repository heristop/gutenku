This haiku captures the essence of unconditional love and forgiveness. The opening line, 'Yet all forgive me,' suggests a sense of redemption or acceptance despite past transgressions. The second line, 'He loved them beyond measure,' emphasizes the depth and boundlessness of this love, hinting at a selfless, perhaps divine, affection. The concluding line, 'Because all is truth,' ties the poem together by suggesting that love and forgiveness are fundamental truths of existence. This haiku invites readers to reflect on the power of love and forgiveness as universal truths that transcend individual faults.
