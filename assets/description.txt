This haiku captures the serene beauty of a distant landscape illuminated by the moonlight. The phrase 'Rising far away' suggests a scene that is both remote and elevated, perhaps a mountain or a hill, creating a sense of awe and wonder. The 'brilliant moonlight' serves as a natural spotlight, enhancing the scene's ethereal quality. The final line, 'As fair as can be,' emphasizes the perfection and beauty of the moment, inviting the reader to appreciate the tranquil and majestic atmosphere. The haiku encapsulates a fleeting yet profound connection with nature's splendor.
