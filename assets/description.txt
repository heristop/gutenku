This haiku captures a fleeting moment of appreciation and contentment. The first line, 'This hath just its time,' suggests the ephemeral nature of the present, emphasizing the importance of savoring each moment. The second line, 'That we have a good repast,' conveys a sense of gratitude for a satisfying meal, symbolizing nourishment and togetherness. The final line, 'Thou lofty heaven,' elevates the experience to a spiritual level, acknowledging the vastness and grandeur of the universe. This haiku beautifully intertwines the simplicity of everyday life with a sense of wonder and reverence for the world beyond.
