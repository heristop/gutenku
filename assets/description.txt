This haiku invites us to consider the beauty and majesty found in moments of stillness and acceptance. The phrase 'If we yield to it' suggests a surrender to nature's tranquility, implying that by embracing calmness, we enhance our own experience of grandeur. The 'tideless mountain lakes' symbolize a serene and unchanging beauty, untouched by the chaos of tides and time. This imagery evokes a sense of peace and eternal majesty, encouraging us to find strength and beauty in stillness and acceptance.
