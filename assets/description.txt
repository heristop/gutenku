This haiku captures the essence of living in the present and the uncertainty of life's journey. The opening line, 'Whither shall we go,' poses a question about direction and purpose, inviting reflection on the paths we choose. The second line, 'A present of things present,' emphasizes the gift of the current moment, suggesting that the present is both a gift and a reality we must embrace. The final line, 'To complete one day,' implies that by focusing on the present, we can find fulfillment and completeness in our daily lives. The haiku encourages mindfulness and the appreciation of the here and now, reminding us that each day is a journey in itself.
