This haiku encapsulates the emotional and existential struggles of a poor man's life. The opening line, "It was weary work," suggests not only the physical toil associated with poverty but also the mental fatigue that accompanies it. The second line, "To be a poor man is hard," directly addresses the harsh realities faced by those without wealth, highlighting the societal and personal challenges they endure. The final line, "To be really true," introduces a profound philosophical reflection; it implies that maintaining authenticity and integrity in the face of adversity is a difficult yet essential task. Together, these lines paint a poignant picture of the intersection between poverty and the quest for truth, emphasizing the weight of both financial and moral struggles.
