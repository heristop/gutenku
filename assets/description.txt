This haiku captures a moment of shared joy and appreciation. The poet observes a collective sense of enjoyment, suggesting that 'they'—perhaps people, animals, or even nature itself—find something very pleasant. The phrase 'The whole big dear world' evokes a sense of unity and affection for the world at large, emphasizing a universal connection through simple pleasures. The haiku's brevity and simplicity mirror the ease with which joy can be found and shared, making it a poignant reflection on the beauty of communal happiness.
