This haiku presents a vivid snapshot of a walrus in a moment of alertness and awareness. The imagery of 'His right arm thrown back' suggests a sense of readiness or surprise, as if the walrus is poised to react. This action sets the stage for the central focus of the haiku: the walrus's gaze. The phrase 'looks about him' conveys a feeling of vigilance and curiosity, emphasizing the creature's engagement with its surroundings. Finally, 'The eyes are large' draws attention to the walrus's keen perception, highlighting its ability to observe and respond to the environment. The haiku captures a moment of stillness and attention, inviting readers to appreciate the walrus's presence and the world it inhabits.
