This haiku presents a serene yet whimsical scene, inviting the reader to ponder the choices we face in moments of tranquility. The first line, 'Which to attend to,' suggests a moment of decision or contemplation, perhaps amidst multiple peaceful options. The second line, 'A beautifully calm race,' introduces a paradoxical image, blending the urgency of a race with the serenity of calmness, highlighting the beauty in balancing life's demands. The final line, 'Taking up biscuits,' adds a touch of humor and domesticity, grounding the abstract contemplation in a simple, everyday pleasure. This haiku captures the essence of finding beauty and humor in the mundane choices of life.
