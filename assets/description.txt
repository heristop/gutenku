This haiku presents a moment of contemplation and decision-making, encapsulated in the imagery of a 'beautifully calm race.' The first line, 'Which to attend to,' suggests a choice or a dilemma, inviting the reader to consider the various paths or options available. The second line, 'A beautifully calm race,' juxtaposes the idea of a race, typically associated with speed and urgency, with calmness, suggesting a serene or mindful approach to life's challenges. The final line, 'Taking up biscuits,' adds a touch of whimsy and domesticity, grounding the abstract decision-making process in a simple, everyday action. This haiku captures the essence of finding peace and beauty in the choices we make, even in the mundane aspects of life.
